About DIT

Learn about our history, mission, and leadership

Overview

Dar es Salaam Institute of Technology (DIT) is a public higher learning institution in Tanzania, established by Act of Parliament No. 6 of 1997. It is a progressive and customer-centered institution dedicated to producing competent engineers, technologists, and technicians who drive Tanzania's industrialization agenda.

DIT is accredited by the National Council for Technical Education (NACTE) and offers programs ranging from Ordinary Diplomas to Master of Engineering degrees.

History

DIT was founded in 1957 as the Dar es Salaam Technical Institute, during a period when Tanzania (then Tanganyika) was building its technical capacity for independence.

Over the decades, it grew in stature and scope, and in 1997, it was formally established by Act of Parliament No. 6 as the Dar es Salaam Institute of Technology.

Today, DIT operates four campuses across Tanzania and continues to be a pillar of technical education in East Africa.

Leadership

The institute is led by a Prof. Preksedis Marco Ndomba who oversees academic and administrative affairs, supported by Deputy Principals for Academic and Administration, a Registrar, and a Finance Officer. DIT's leadership is committed to maintaining excellence in technical education.

Accreditation & Partnerships

Accreditation

  • National Council for Technical Education (NACTE)
  • Tanzania Commission for Universities (TCU) – for degree programs
  • Engineers Registration Board (ERB) – for engineering programs

Partnerships

  • Ministry of Education, Science and Technology
  • Tanzania Industrial Research and Development Organization (TIRDO)
  • Private sector industries for internships and training
  • International technical institutions

Campuses Overview

DIT operates four campuses across Tanzania:

  • Main Campus – Dar es Salaam (Administration & Engineering)
  • Mwanza Campus – Laboratory Science & Leather Technology
  • Songwe Campus (Myunga) – Civil, Plumbing & ICT
  • Dodoma Campus – Skills development for vocational graduates
